# Recruiting Assistance Rules

## Role Understanding
- Internal recruiter vs agency recruiter have different incentives â€” agencies paid on placement, internal on fit
- Recruiting is sales with humans â€” both selling the role to candidates and candidates to hiring managers
- Time-to-hire matters but quality matters more â€” bad hires cost 2-3x salary to fix

## Job Descriptions
- Requirements vs nice-to-haves must be clearly separated â€” overloaded requirements scare good candidates
- "Years of experience" is a poor proxy â€” focus on demonstrated skills and outcomes
- Salary range transparency attracts better candidates â€” hiding it wastes everyone's time
- Avoid gendered language and unnecessary requirements â€” "rockstar" and "must lift 50lbs" narrow pools
- Include what the role actually does daily â€” not just responsibilities, but reality

## Sourcing Candidates
- LinkedIn is obvious but saturated â€” good candidates get 50+ messages weekly
- Personalized outreach beats templates â€” reference specific work, projects, or posts
- Referrals have higher success rates â€” incentivize and track them
- Passive candidates need compelling reasons â€” they're not looking, give them a reason to listen
- Diverse sourcing requires intentional effort â€” same channels produce same candidate profiles

## Resume Screening
- Look for impact, not just responsibilities â€” "managed team" vs "grew team from 3 to 12"
- Job hopping context matters â€” industry, company health, growth trajectory
- Gaps aren't automatic disqualifiers â€” ask, don't assume
- Side projects and open source show initiative â€” especially for technical roles
- Education requirements exclude capable candidates â€” evaluate when truly necessary

## Interview Process
- Define evaluation criteria before interviews â€” consistency enables comparison
- Structured interviews reduce bias â€” same questions, same order, scoring rubric
- Take-home tests respect candidate time limits â€” 2-4 hours max, paid if longer
- Interview loops shouldn't exceed 4-5 rounds â€” respect for candidates' time signals culture
- Interviewers need training â€” untrained interviewers make poor decisions and create legal risk

## Candidate Experience
- Respond to all applicants â€” ghosting damages employer brand
- Communicate timeline upfront â€” "you'll hear back in X days" then actually do it
- Feedback after rejection helps when possible â€” legal concerns are often overstated
- Keep candidates warm during slow processes â€” silence feels like rejection
- Rejected candidates may return or refer â€” treat everyone as future relationship

## Compensation
- Know market rates for the role â€” underpaying attracts desperate, not best
- Total compensation includes equity, benefits, flexibility â€” don't just compare base
- Negotiation isn't adversarial â€” find mutually acceptable terms
- Internal equity matters â€” new hires shouldn't out-earn existing employees unfairly
- Be prepared to walk away â€” desperation leads to bad deals

## Pipeline Management
- Track candidates through stages â€” lost candidates are wasted sourcing effort
- Speed matters â€” good candidates have options, slow processes lose them
- Bottlenecks are usually hiring manager availability â€” surface and solve
- Rejection reasons inform future sourcing â€” pattern recognition improves targeting
- Keep pipelines warm for future roles â€” relationship building pays off

## Legal Considerations
- Consistent process protects against discrimination claims â€” document everything
- Questions about age, family, religion, disability are off-limits â€” focus on job requirements
- Background checks require consent and compliance â€” laws vary by jurisdiction
- Offer letters should be reviewed by legal â€” verbal offers can create issues
- Non-competes and NDAs in offers need explanation â€” candidates should understand

## Red Flags in Candidates
- Badmouthing previous employers â€” pattern will continue
- Vague answers about accomplishments â€” may not have been responsible
- Inability to explain gaps or moves â€” evasion suggests problems
- Different story in different interviews â€” consistency matters
- Unwillingness to provide references â€” hiding something

## Common Mistakes
- Hiring for culture fit that's actually bias â€” "fit" can exclude diversity
- Rushing to fill headcount â€” empty seat better than wrong person
- Ignoring hiring manager feedback patterns â€” some reject everyone, some accept everyone
- Not selling the role actively â€” interviewing is two-way evaluation
- Over-relying on credentials â€” pedigree doesn't guarantee performance

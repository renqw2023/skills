# CEO Support Rules

## Decision Making
- Reversible decisions fast, irreversible decisions slow â€” don't over-analyze what you can undo
- Decide with 70% information â€” waiting for 100% means competitors moved first
- Write down the decision criteria before evaluating options â€” prevents post-hoc rationalization
- Ask "what would have to be true" for each option to work â€” surfaces assumptions
- Disagree and commit is valid â€” endless consensus-seeking kills momentum

## Prioritization
- Only 3 priorities maximum â€” more than 3 means no priorities
- Default answer to new commitments is no â€” protect focus ruthlessly
- Urgent vs important: schedule important before urgent crowds it out
- Weekly review: are daily actions aligned with quarterly goals? â€” easy to drift
- Kill projects that aren't working â€” sunk cost is sunk, future cost isn't

## Time Management
- Calendar audit: does time allocation match stated priorities? â€” track for a week
- Batch similar activities â€” context switching has hidden cost
- Buffer time between meetings â€” back-to-back destroys thinking capacity
- Protect deep work blocks â€” some problems need 4 hours, not 4 one-hour slots
- Delegate anything someone else can do 80% as well â€” your 20% improvement isn't worth it

## Communication
- State the ask first, then context â€” busy people need the point upfront
- Written updates over status meetings â€” async scales, meetings don't
- Bad news travels up immediately â€” never let the CEO be surprised
- Summarize decisions and owners after every meeting â€” confusion kills execution
- Over-communicate strategy, under-communicate tactics â€” teams need why, not just what

## People
- Hire slow, fire fast â€” bad fits get worse, not better
- A-players attract A-players, B-players hire C-players â€” first hires set the bar
- Direct feedback immediately â€” delayed feedback is useless feedback
- Trust but verify early, trust fully later â€” new hires need support, veterans need autonomy
- Culture is what you tolerate, not what you proclaim â€” behavior you ignore becomes policy

## Metrics
- Pick 1-3 metrics that matter, ignore vanity metrics â€” revenue, retention, and one more
- Leading indicators over lagging â€” measure what predicts outcomes, not just outcomes
- Dashboard review weekly, deep dive monthly â€” rhythm matters more than frequency
- If you can't measure it, at least define what success looks like â€” qualitative goals need criteria too
- Benchmarks provide context â€” your 5% might be great or terrible depending on industry

- Present problems with proposed solutions â€” boards advise, not solve
- Cash runway is the only metric that kills you â€” know it exactly, always
- Update investors monthly even when things are hard â€” silence destroys trust
- Ask board members for specific help â€” vague asks get vague help

- Competitive advantage erodes â€” what's defensible in 3 years, not just today
- Customer obsession over competitor obsession â€” build what users need, not what rivals have
- Simple strategy everyone understands beats complex strategy nobody executes
